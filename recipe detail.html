Kabuli Palio-Full recipe

Ingredients:

  • Basmati Rice: The foundation of the dish, known for its long grains and fragrant aroma.
  • Lamb or Chicken: Commonly used for protein; can be substituted with beef or other meats based on preference.
  • Onions: Usually sliced and saut√©ed until golden brown for added flavor.
  • Carrots: Julienne carrots are often saut√©ed and caramelized to add sweetness and color.
  • Raisins: These provide a sweet contrast to the savory elements of the dish.
  • Almonds or Pistachios: Chopped or whole nuts for added texture and flavor.
  • Garlic: Often used for seasoning, enhancing the overall flavor.
  • Spices: A blend that typically includes cumin, cardamom, cinnamon, and sometimes black pepper.
  • Salt: For seasoning.
  • Ghee or Oil: Used for cooking and to enhance the richness of the dish.

Step- by- step instruction for Kabuli Pulio:

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Wash the Rice: Rinse 2 cups of basmati rice under cold running water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch.
    • Soak the Rice: Soak the rinsed rice in water for at least 30 minutes. Drain before cooking.
  2. Cook the Meat:
    • Heat Ghee/Oil: In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil over medium heat.
    • Saut√© Onions: Add the sliced onions and cook until they are golden brown.
    • Add Meat: Add the lamb or chicken pieces to the pot and brown on all sides.
    • Garlic and Spices: Stir in the minced garlic (if using), cumin seeds, cardamom pods, and cinnamon. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are fragrant.
    • Add Water: Pour in 4 cups of water, season with salt and pepper, and bring to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer.
    • Cook Meat: Cover and cook until the meat is tender (about 30-40 minutes for chicken and about 1-1.5 hours for lamb). Adjust cooking time based on the type of meat used.
  3. Prepare Toppings:
    • Saut√© Carrots: In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or oil. Add the julienned carrots and saut√© until slightly caramelized (about 5-7 minutes).
    • Add Raisins: Add raisins to the pan with the carrots and cook for another couple of minutes until they swell. Remove from heat and set aside.
    • Toast Nuts: In the same or another dry pan, lightly toast the slivered almonds or pistachios until golden. Set aside.
  4. Cook the Rice:
    • Add Soaked Rice: Once the meat is tender, remove it from the pot and set aside. Strain the cooking liquid to separate any solids.
    • Combine Rice and Broth: In the same pot with the strained broth, bring it back to a boil. Add the drained basmati rice into the pot.
    • Adjust Seasoning: If you like, add saffron dissolved in a little warm water for color and flavor.
    • Cook Rice: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and reduce the heat to low. Cook for about 20-25 minutes until the rice is fluffy and fully cooked. Avoid opening the lid during this time.
  5. Assemble the Dish
    • Layer the Dish: Once the rice is cooked, gently fluff it with a fork and layer the meat back on top.
    • Add Toppings: Arrange the saut√©ed carrots with raisins and toasted nuts over the rice.
    • Final Garnish: Optional ‚Äì drizzle a bit of melted ghee over the top for extra richness.
  6. Serve
    • Serve Hot: Gently mix the layers together before serving, if desired, and serve hot, ideally with a side of yogurt or salad.

Afghan Dumpling(Mantu)-Full recipe:

Ingredients:

For the Doogh:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed)

For the Filling:

  • 500 g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley or cilantro (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

For the Sauce:

  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional)

Step by Step instruction for Mantu(Afghan Dumpling):

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • Mix Ingredients: In a bowl, combine flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead until you have a smooth dough.
    • Rest: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and allow it to rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • Combine Filling Ingredients: In a bowl, mix the ground meat, chopped onion, chopped parsley/cilantro, cumin, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Shape the Dumplings:
    • Roll Out Dough: Divide the rested dough into small balls, then roll each ball out into thin circles (about 4 inches in diameter).
    • Fill Dumplings: Place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough circle.
    • Seal: Fold the dough over the filling and pinch the edges to seal tightly, creating a half-moon shape or a pleated dumpling shape.
  4. Cook the Dumplings:
    • Steam: Arrange the dumplings in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper or greased. Steam them over boiling water for about 30-40 minutes until cooked through.
  5. Prepare the Sauce:
    • Mix Sauce Ingredients: In a bowl, combine yogurt, minced garlic, and a pinch of salt. You can also drizzle olive oil or melted butter and sprinkle paprika on top.
  6. Serve
    • Plate: Serve the steamed mantu on a platter, drizzled with the yogurt sauce. Optionally, garnish with extra herbs or a sprinkle of paprika.

  • 2 cups chopped spinach or other leafy greens
  • 1 cup carrots, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, cut into small pieces
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced (or 1 can of diced tomatoes)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley or cilantro (optional, for garnish)

For the Yogurt Sauce:

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

    • Step by step instruction for Afghan Noodle(Aush)

    1. Make the Noodles:
      • Combine Flour and Egg: In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a well in the center and add the egg.
      • Mix: Gradually mix the egg into the flour using a fork. Add water a little at a time until you form a dough.
      • Knead: Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
      • Roll and Cut: Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thick. Cut into wide strips (about 1/2 inch wide).
    2. Prepare the Soup:
      • Brown the Meat: In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until translucent. Add the ground meat and cook until browned.
      • Add Vegetables: Stir in the diced tomatoes, carrots, and green beans. Cook for a few minutes.
      • Add Broth: Pour in the broth (or water) and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to simmer.
      • Season: Add turmeric, paprika, salt, and pepper. Let the soup simmer for about 20 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

      • Cook Noodles: In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add the fresh noodles and cook for about 4-5 minutes or until al dente. Drain and set aside.
    4. Serve:

      • Serve: In deep bowls, place a portion of the cooked noodles, then ladle the soup over them.

Afghani-Chicken-Kebab-Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (about 900 g) boneless chicken (thighs or breasts), cut into cubes
  • 1 large onion, finely grated
  • 4-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional for color)
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • Wood or metal skewers (if using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • Gather Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the grated onion, minced garlic, yogurt, vegetable oil, lemon juice, ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric, paprika, salt, and pepper.
    • Add Chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring all pieces are thoroughly coated.
    • Marinate: Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight for better flavor.
  2. Preheat and Prepare:
    • Preheat the Grill: If using a charcoal grill, light the coals and allow them to burn until they are covered with ash. If using a gas grill, preheat on medium-high heat.
    • Thread Chicken onto Skewers: Remove the marinated chicken from the fridge. Thread the chicken pieces onto the skewers, leaving some space between each piece for even cooking.
  3. Grill the Kebabs
    • Oil the Grate: Lightly oil the grill grate to prevent sticking.
    • Grill the Kebabs: Place the skewers on the grill. Cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has nice grill marks. The internal temperature should reach at least 165¬∞F (75¬∞C).
  4. Serve:
    • Remove from Grill: Once cooked, carefully remove the kebabs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.
    • Garnish: Serve the kebabs with fresh herbs (like cilantro or parsley), lemon wedges, and traditional accompaniments such as naan bread, rice, or a fresh salad.

    Optional Side Dishes:

    • Aush (Afghan Noodle Soup)
    • A simple salad with tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions

Qorma(Stew)-Full recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900 g) lamb or beef, cut into chunks (you can use chicken as well)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2-3 tomatoes, chopped (or 1 can of diced tomatoes)
  • 2-3 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or ghee
  • 4 cups water or broth (beef or chicken)
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley (for garnish)

Step-by-Step for Qorma

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Chop Vegetables: Finely chop the onion and mince the garlic. Chop the tomatoes and slice the carrots and potatoes (if using).
    • Cut the Meat: Cut the meat into bite-sized chunks if not already done.
  2. Saut√© the Onions
    • Heat Oil: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil or ghee over medium heat.
    • Saut√© Onions: Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they are golden brown (about 8-10 minutes). Stir frequently to prevent burning.
  3. Add Meat and Spices:
    • Add Meat: Add the meat chunks to the pot with the browned onions.
    • Season: Stir in the minced garlic, cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika (if using), salt, and black pepper. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the meat is browned on all sides.
  4. Add Vegetables and Liquid:
    • Add Tomatoes: Stir in the chopped tomatoes and cook for a few minutes until they soften.
    • Add Carrots and Potatoes: If using, add the sliced carrots and diced potatoes.
    • Pour in Water/Broth: Add enough water or broth to cover the meat and vegetables (about 4 cups). Bring to a boil.
  5. Simmer the Qorma
    • Reduce Heat: Once it reaches a boil, reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours. This allows the flavors to meld and the meat to become tender.
  6. Final Adjustments
    • Check Seasoning: After simmering, taste the Qorma and adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper as needed.
    • Thicken the Stew: If you prefer a thicker consistency, you can remove the lid for the last 15-20 minutes of cooking to allow some of the liquid to evaporate.
  7. Serve:
    • Garnish: Once cooked, remove from heat and garnish with freshly chopped cilantro or parsley.
    • Serve Hot: Qorma is typically served hot over steamed rice or with Afghan bread (naan). Accompany it with a fresh salad or yogurt.

Flat Bread(Bolani)-Full recipe

Ingredients:

For Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 to 1.5 cups warm water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (optional)

For Potato filling:

  • 3-4 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1-2 green onions, finely chopped (or 1 small onion)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Step by step instruction:

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and sugar. Mix well.
    • Add Water: Gradually add warm water, mixing with your hand or a wooden spoon until the mixture comes together into a dough.
    • Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic. If desired, add vegetable oil while kneading for extra softness.
    • Rest the Dough: Cover the dough with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Potato Filling;
    • Boil the Potatoes: Place the cubed potatoes in a pot of boiling salted water. Cook until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and let them cool slightly.
    • Mash the Potatoes: In a bowl, mash the cooked potatoes until smooth.
    • Add Ingredients: Stir in the chopped green onions, cilantro or parsley, cumin powder (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix well and set aside to cool.
  3. Assemble the Bolani:
    • Divide the Dough: After resting, divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball (approximately 8-10 balls).
    • Roll Out Dough: Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly. Using a rolling pin, roll it out into a circle about 8-10 inches in diameter.
    • Add Filling: Place a generous spoonful of the potato filling onto one half of the rolled dough, leaving some space at the edges.
    • Fold the Dough: Fold the other half of the dough over the filling to create a semi-circle. Use your fingers to press the edges together, sealing it tightly. You can also crimp the edges for a decorative touch.
  4. Cook the Bolani:
    • Heat the Pan: In a large skillet or frying pan, heat a few tablespoons of oil over medium heat.
    • Fry the Bolani: Carefully place the bolani in the hot oil. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden and crispy. Adjust the heat as necessary to avoid burning.
    • Drain Excess Oil: Once cooked, remove the bolani from the skillet and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve Warm: Cut the bolani into wedges and serve warm. It can be enjoyed by itself or with yogurt, chutney, or salad on the side.
